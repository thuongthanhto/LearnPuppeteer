What is DTI and How Do I Calculate it?
DTI stands for Debt-to-Income and allows us to determine the repayment ability of the client. There are two kinds of Debt Ratios: Housing (Front-end) Ratio and Total (Back-end) Ratio

  • The housing ratio, or front-end ratio, is all debts related to the property that the client is obligated to pay on a monthly basis divided by the gross monthly income.
    • Calculation: PITIA Payment /GMI
  • The total ratio, or the back end ratio, is all of the client's debts, including housing expenses and other miscellaneous debts, divided by the gross monthly income.
    • Calculation: Total Debt / GMI
